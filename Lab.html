Games


The role that games play in our culture is peculiar. Most people have participated in a game at some point in their lives. Despite their widespread use, video games are rarely treated with the same respect as other forms of media like books or films. For the majority, playing video games is like eating chocolate: it's a secret vice. A game, according to the game designer Sid Meier, is "a series of interesting options," which we navigate to choose how the game will play out and reveal our personalities and thought processes. Playing a game is exposition in action.

Movies


The ability to experience a range of emotions while watching a movie is one of the reasons we like it so much. These include excitement, joy, sadness, intensity, comfort, shock, and nervousness. They enable us to empathize with imaginary characters we've never met before as we watch them struggle, recover, and finally save the day. Films always manage to brighten us up, which is another reason why we adore them. If you feel down, just turn on a happy and cheery cartoon movie to lighten your mood. Likewise, if you need to laugh, just put on an entertaining comedy film.
